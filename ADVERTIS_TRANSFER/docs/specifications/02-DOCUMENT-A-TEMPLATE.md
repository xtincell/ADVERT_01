### 1.1 Brand Essence Statement

**Format:** Single sentence that captures the irreducible core

**Template:**
```yaml
brand_essence:
  statement: "[One sentence capturing the irreducible truth of the brand]" *

  components:
    core_truth: "[What is fundamentally true]"
    unique_position: "[What makes this distinct]"
    emotional_resonance: "[Why people care]"

  test_criteria:
    - "If we changed this, would we still be us?"
    - "Does this guide decisions?"
    - "Is this ownable and distinct?"

  examples:
    good:
      - "Nike: Authentic athletic performance that unleashes human potential"
      - "Patagonia: Environmental activism disguised as outdoor apparel"
      - "Apple: Beautifully designed tools for creative rebels"

    bad:
      - "We are a leading provider of quality products" (generic, not essence)
      - "Customer satisfaction is our priority" (aspiration, not truth)
```

### 2.1 Purpose Statement

**Format:** Clear, inspiring statement of why the brand exists beyond profit

**Template:**
```yaml
purpose:
  statement: "[Why we exist beyond making money - 1-2 sentences]" *

  elaboration: |
    [2-3 paragraphs explaining the purpose in depth]

    Paragraph 1: The problem or gap in the world that called this brand into existence
    Paragraph 2: How the brand uniquely addresses this problem
    Paragraph 3: The world-changing impact if the brand succeeds

  proof_points: *
    - proof_1:
        claim: "[Evidence this purpose is real]"
        example: "[Specific instance demonstrating this]"

    - proof_2:
        claim: "[Evidence this purpose is real]"
        example: "[Specific instance demonstrating this]"

    - proof_3:
        claim: "[Evidence this purpose is real]"
        example: "[Specific instance demonstrating this]"

  purpose_test:
    simon_sinek_why: "[The fundamental WHY]"
    problem_solved: "[If we didn't exist, what would be worse?]"
    stakeholder_benefit: "[Who benefits beyond shareholders?]"

  common_mistakes:
    avoid:
      - "To be the leading..." (aspiration, not purpose)
      - "To provide quality..." (means, not purpose)
      - "To maximize shareholder value" (consequence, not purpose)
```

**Example (Patagonia):**
```yaml
purpose:
  statement: "We're in business to save our home planet."

  elaboration: |
    The outdoor places we cherish are under threat from climate change,
    habitat destruction, and unsustainable consumption. We believe business
    can be a force for environmental solutions, not just problems.

    Patagonia uses business to prove that capitalism can coexist with
    environmental responsibility. Every product, every decision, every dollar
    is evaluated against its impact on the planet.

    If we succeed, we'll have demonstrated a new model of business that
    prioritizes planetary health alongside profit, inspiring other companies
    to do the same.

  proof_points:
    - claim: "1% for the Planet commitment since 1985"
      example: "Over $140M donated to environmental organizations"

    - claim: "Worn Wear program reduces consumption"
      example: "Repairs 80,000+ garments annually instead of selling new"

    - claim: "100% renewable energy in owned facilities"
      example: "Solar installations, carbon-neutral operations"
```

```yaml
    - behavior_2: "[Specific action that demonstrates this value]"
    - behavior_3: "[Specific action that demonstrates this value]"
    - behavior_4: "[Specific action that demonstrates this value]"

    - anti_1: "[What this value is NOT / What violates this value]"

  real_world_examples: *

    - example_2:

        action: "[How we applied the value]"
        outcome: "[Result of living the value]"

  hiring_filter: "[How we assess this value in candidates]"

  decision_test: "[Question to ask when facing decisions related to this value]"
```

**Example (Netflix - "Freedom & Responsibility"):**
```yaml
value_1:
  name: "Freedom & Responsibility"

  definition: |
    We give people freedom to make decisions and take informed risks,
    while holding them accountable for results. We don't control with
    rules; we control by hiring responsible people and creating context.

  why_this_value: |
    Innovation requires freedom to experiment and fail. Bureaucracy kills
    creativity. We trust talented people to do right by Netflix without
    needing approval for every decision. This attracts top talent and
    enables fast adaptation in a rapidly changing entertainment landscape.

  observable_behaviors:
    - "No vacation policy - people take time when they need it"
    - "No expense approval process - people use good judgment"
    - "Managers provide context, not control"
    - "Employees make decisions without asking permission"
    - "We measure on outcomes, not process compliance"

  anti_patterns:
    - "Freedom without accountability (chaos)"
    - "Responsibility without freedom (micromanagement)"
    - "Hiding mistakes instead of learning from them"
    - "Blaming process instead of taking ownership"

  real_world_examples:
    - situation: "Engineer wants to try new technology stack"
      action: "No approval needed - they researched, made case, implemented"
      outcome: "Innovation faster, ownership higher, mistakes learned from"

    - situation: "Someone abused unlimited vacation policy"
      action: "Addressed with individual, didn't create rules for everyone"
      outcome: "Kept freedom intact while ensuring accountability"

  hiring_filter: "Do they have a track record of good judgment? Can they
                  handle ambiguity? Do they take ownership of outcomes?"

  decision_test: "If everyone made this decision independently with full
                  context, would we get the outcome we want?"
```

**Example (Airbnb):**
```yaml
brand_story:
  heros_journey_structure:

      description: |

        authentic cultural connections.
      pain_points:
        - "Expensive, impersonal hotel experiences"
        - "No connection to local culture"
        - "Homeowners with unused space, no income"

    call_to_adventure:
      catalyst: |
        2007, San Francisco. Brian Chesky and Joe Gebbia couldn't afford
        rent during a design conference. They bought air mattresses,
        rented space to conference attendees, provided breakfast.
      insight: "People crave authentic local experiences and are willing
               to trust strangers if the platform creates safety."
      founder_moment: "Three air mattresses + breakfast = AirBedAndBreakfast.com"

    trials_and_challenges:
      - challenge_1:
          obstacle: "Nobody believed strangers would let strangers into homes"
          how_overcome: "Built trust systems: reviews, verified photos,

      - challenge_2:
          obstacle: "Hotel industry lobbying, regulations, legal battles"
          how_overcome: "Grassroots community advocacy, economic impact
                         studies, worked with cities"

      - challenge_3:
          obstacle: "Scaling trust globally across cultures"
          how_overcome: "Localized approach, community standards, 24/7 support"

    transformation:
      what_changed: |
        Travel industry permanently changed. 'Home-sharing' became mainstream.
        Millions earn income from homes. Travelers expect authentic local
        experiences. 'Belonging' became core to hospitality.
      proof_of_change:
        - "7M+ listings in 220+ countries"
        - "Enabled $100B+ in host earnings"
        - "'Airbnb' became a verb"
      new_reality: "A world where anyone can belong anywhere"

    the_return:
      impact: "Democratized travel. Empowered local economies. Created
               cultural exchange at scale."
      ongoing_mission: "Expanding to experiences, long-term stays, full
                        trip planning"
      invitation: "Become a host. Travel like a local. Belong anywhere."
```

### 7.1 Schwartz Values Mapping

**Format:** Plot brand on universal human values framework

**Template:**
```yaml
schwartz_values_mapping:

  framework_overview:
    description: |
      Schwartz Theory of Basic Values identifies 10 universal values
      organized in a circular structure showing relationships and conflicts.

    ten_values:
      - Self-Direction: Independent thought and action
      - Stimulation: Excitement, novelty, challenge
      - Hedonism: Pleasure and sensual gratification
      - Achievement: Personal success through competence
      - Power: Social status and prestige, control
      - Security: Safety, harmony, stability
      - Conformity: Restraint of actions violating social norms
      - Tradition: Respect for customs and ideas of culture
      - Benevolence: Preserving and enhancing welfare of close others
      - Universalism: Understanding, appreciation, tolerance for all

  brand_values_plotted: *
    primary_values:
      - value_name: "[One of 10 Schwartz values]"
        strength: "[High/Medium/Low]"
        manifestation: "[How this shows up in brand]"

    secondary_values:
      - value_name: "[Supporting value]"
        strength: "[Medium/Low]"
        manifestation: "[How this shows up in brand]"

    conflicting_values:
      - value_name: "[Value in tension with primary]"
        tension: "[How brand navigates this conflict]"

  values_priorities: *
    rank_1: "[Most important Schwartz value]"
    rank_2: "[Second most important]"
    rank_3: "[Third most important]"

    why_this_order: |
      [Explanation of why these values are prioritized this way]
```

**Example (Patagonia):**
```yaml
schwartz_values_mapping:

  brand_values_plotted:
    primary_values:
      - value_name: "Universalism"
        strength: "High"

                        responsibility. '1% for the Planet' commitment."

      - value_name: "Self-Direction"
        strength: "High"
        manifestation: "Independent action, unconventional business model,

    secondary_values:
      - value_name: "Benevolence"
```

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

**Generic Purpose:**
- ‚ùå "To be the leading provider of..."
- ‚úÖ "To [specific impact on world beyond profit]"

**Aspirational Values (not real):**
- ‚ùå "We value innovation" (everyone says this)
- ‚úÖ "Question Everything" (specific, behavioral, ownable)

**Too Many Values:**
- ‚ùå 8-10 values (dilution, not memorable)
- ‚úÖ 3-5 values (focused, memorable, actionable)

**Values Without Behaviors:**
- ‚ùå "Integrity" (abstract, not actionable)
- ‚úÖ "Integrity: We tell clients what they need to hear, not what they want to hear, even if we lose the deal"

**Story That's Just History:**
- ‚ùå "Founded in 1987, we started with..."
- ‚úÖ "The world needed X. We discovered Y. Now Z is possible."
