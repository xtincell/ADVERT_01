### 2.2 Risk Assessment Methodology

**Format:** Clear, repeatable risk evaluation framework

**Template:**
```yaml
risk_assessment_methodology:

  risk_identification_process:
    sources: *
      - "Leadership interviews (strategic perspective)"
      - "Team workshops (operational insights)"
      - "Customer feedback analysis (external perception)"
      - "Competitive intelligence (market threats)"
      - "Industry reports (macro trends)"
      - "Historical incident analysis (past problems)"

    categories:
      - "Strategic (market, competitive, business model)"
      - "Operational (technology, supply chain, financial, people)"
      - "Reputational (brand, social, ESG, crisis)"
      - "Compliance (regulatory, legal, IP)"

  risk_evaluation_criteria:

    impact_scale: *
      level_5_catastrophic:
        description: "Existential threat to brand/business"
        examples: "Brand collapse, bankruptcy, criminal liability"
        financial: ">$10M loss or >50% revenue decline"
        reputational: "Irreparable brand damage, loss of license to operate"

      level_4_major:
        description: "Severe damage requiring years to recover"
        examples: "Major product recall, executive scandal, data breach"
        financial: "$1M-$10M loss or 20-50% revenue decline"
        reputational: "National media coverage, significant customer loss"

      level_3_moderate:
        description: "Significant disruption, recoverable in 6-12 months"
        examples: "Service outage, supplier failure, local controversy"
        financial: "$100K-$1M loss or 5-20% revenue impact"
        reputational: "Industry coverage, some customer complaints"

      level_2_minor:
        description: "Noticeable impact, quickly resolved"
        examples: "Minor quality issue, small vendor problem"
        financial: "$10K-$100K loss or <5% revenue impact"
        reputational: "Limited social media mentions"

      level_1_negligible:
        description: "Minimal impact, routine to handle"
        examples: "Single customer complaint, minor process hiccup"
        financial: "<$10K loss"
        reputational: "No public visibility"

    probability_scale: *
      level_5_certain:
        description: "Will definitely occur (>90% chance)"
        frequency: "Multiple times per year"
        evidence: "Already happening regularly"

      level_4_likely:
        description: "High probability (60-90% chance)"
        frequency: "Annually or more"
        evidence: "Strong indicators present"

      level_3_possible:
        description: "Could happen (30-60% chance)"
        frequency: "Every 2-5 years"
        evidence: "Some indicators, has happened to peers"

      level_2_unlikely:
        description: "Low probability (10-30% chance)"
        frequency: "Every 5-10 years"
        evidence: "Rare occurrence in industry"

      level_1_rare:
        description: "Highly unlikely (<10% chance)"
        frequency: "Once in 10+ years or never"
        evidence: "No precedent or indicators"

    risk_score_calculation:
      formula: "Risk Score = Probability (1-5) √ó Impact (1-5)"
      range: "1 (lowest) to 25 (highest)"

  risk_priority_zones: *
    critical_risks:
      score_range: "20-25"
      action: "Immediate mitigation required, board-level oversight"
      review_frequency: "Weekly monitoring"

    high_risks:
      score_range: "12-19"
      action: "Active mitigation, executive oversight"
      review_frequency: "Monthly monitoring"

    medium_risks:
      score_range: "6-11"
      action: "Mitigation plans in place, regular monitoring"
      review_frequency: "Quarterly review"

    low_risks:
      score_range: "1-5"
      action: "Accept or monitor, minimal active mitigation"
      review_frequency: "Annual review"
```

  risk_id: "REP-01"

  description: |
    A brand action, statement, product, or employee behavior sparks outrage

    mainstream media coverage. The crisis damages brand reputation, customer
    trust, and potentially impacts sales.

    This could be triggered by perceived insensitivity, hypocrisy (actions
    contradicting stated values), cultural appropriation, tone-deaf marketing,
    or association with controversial figures/causes. The 24/7 social media
    environment means crises can escalate in hours, not days.

    If occurs: Hashtag campaigns, influencer criticism, media scrutiny, customer
    defection, employee morale damage, potential long-term brand equity loss.

  probability:
    rating: 4 (Likely - 60-90% chance)
    rationale: "Social media crises are common in our industry. Competitors
                have faced similar issues. Our brand takes values-driven
                stances, which can be polarizing. High brand visibility
                increases scrutiny."
    trend: "Increasing (social media sensitivity growing)"

  impact:
    rating: 4 (Major - severe damage)
    rationale: "Could cause 20-40% sales decline during crisis, lose key
                partnerships, damage employer brand. Recovery takes 12-24 months.
                Some customers lost permanently."
    affected_areas:
      - "Revenue (short-term sales impact)"
      - "Brand reputation (long-term equity damage)"
      - "Customer loyalty (trust erosion)"
      - "Employee morale (pride in brand)"
      - "Partnership relationships (brand safety concerns)"

  risk_score: "16 (Probability 4 √ó Impact 4 = HIGH RISK)"

  triggers:
    - "Marketing campaign deemed insensitive or appropriative"
    - "Executive makes controversial public statement"
    - "Product defect causes harm and response seems inadequate"
    - "Brand takes stance on divisive social/political issue"
    - "Employee behavior reflects poorly on company"

  indicators:
    leading_indicators:

      - indicator: "Influencer/celebrity criticism"

        measurement: "Media monitoring"

        measurement: "Daily sales reports"

  mitigation_strategies:

        owner: "CMO + Chief Brand Officer"
        timeline: "Ongoing (mandatory pre-launch)"
        cost: "Internal resource time, external consultants $50k/year"

      - strategy_2: "Diverse review panel for creative work"
        owner: "Creative Director"
        timeline: "Q1 2026 implementation"
        cost: "$30k for panel recruitment + compensation"

      - strategy_3: "Executive social media training and protocols"
        owner: "Chief Communications Officer"
        timeline: "Q1 2026"
        cost: "$20k training program"

      - strategy_4: "Social listening 24/7 monitoring"
        owner: "Social Media Director"
        timeline: "Already in place"
        cost: "$120k/year tools + staff"

    reduce_impact:
      - strategy_1: "Crisis communication playbook (see Section 8.3)"
        owner: "Chief Communications Officer"
        timeline: "Completed"

        owner: "PR team"
        timeline: "Q1 2026"

      - strategy_3: "Brand advocates network (employees, loyal customers)"
        owner: "Community Manager"
        timeline: "Q2 2026 launch"
        cost: "$80k program budget"
