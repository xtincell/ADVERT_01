### Purpose
Document V articulates exactly what value the brand delivers, to whom, and why that value is unique and compelling. It bridges the internal truth (Doc A) with external market realities, defining how the brand positions itself in the competitive landscape.

### Audience
- Leadership Team (strategy decisions)
- Marketing & Sales Teams (messaging foundation)
- Product Team (feature prioritization)
- Customer Success (value realization)
- Investors (market opportunity validation)

### Relationship to Other Documents
Document V **positions** the brand strategically:
- Built on Pillar A (authentic purpose)
- Expressed through Pillar D (visual differentiation)
- Activated by Pillar E (campaign messaging)
- Measured by Pillar T (value delivery metrics)

### 2.1 Positioning Statement

**Format:** Classic positioning statement structure

**Template:**
```yaml
positioning_statement:

  complete_statement: |
    For [target audience]
    Who [need/problem statement]
    [Brand name] is the [category/frame of reference]
    That [key benefit/point of difference]
    Unlike [competitors/alternatives], we [unique differentiator/reason to believe]

  components:

    target_audience: *
      segment: "[Specific audience segment]"
      characteristics: "[Key defining traits]"

    need_problem: *
      core_need: "[Job-to-be-done]"
      current_frustration: "[What's not working with alternatives]"

    category_frame: *
      primary_category: "[Category we compete in]"
      reframe_opportunity: "[How we might redefine the category]"

    key_benefit: *
      benefit_statement: "[Single most important benefit]"
      why_it_matters: "[Impact on customer's life/business]"

    differentiation_rtb: *
      unique_attribute: "[What we have/do that others don't]"
      proof_point: "[Why customers should believe us]"

  positioning_rationale: |
    [2-3 paragraphs explaining why this positioning]

    Paragraph 1: Why this target audience
    Paragraph 2: Why this benefit resonates
    Paragraph 3: Why this differentiator is ownable and defensible

  tagline_connection:
    tagline: "[Brand tagline if exists]"
    relationship: "[How tagline expresses positioning]"
```

**Example (Tesla):**
```yaml
positioning_statement:

  complete_statement: |
    For environmentally conscious consumers
    Who want high-performance vehicles without compromising the planet
    Tesla is the sustainable automotive company
    That delivers exhilarating electric vehicles with superior technology
    Unlike traditional automakers focused on incremental hybrid improvements,
    we're accelerating the world's transition to sustainable energy through
    groundbreaking battery technology and software innovation.

  components:

    target_audience:
      segment: "Affluent early adopters (HHI $150k+, age 35-55)"
      characteristics: "Environmentally conscious, tech-forward, performance-oriented,
                        willing to pay premium for innovation and values alignment"

    need_problem:
      core_need: "Drive a high-performance vehicle while reducing environmental impact"
      current_frustration: "EVs were slow/ugly/limited range. Hybrids were compromises.
                            Gas cars pollute but perform."

    category_frame:
      primary_category: "Automotive (specifically EV category leader)"
      reframe_opportunity: "Not just 'electric cars' but 'sustainable energy company'
                            - cars, solar, batteries, energy storage"

    key_benefit:
      benefit_statement: "Exhilarating performance + environmental responsibility
                          (0-60 in 2s + zero emissions)"
      why_it_matters: "Don't have to choose between fun and planet. Can have both."

    differentiation_rtb:
      unique_attribute: "Superior battery technology (range, charging speed, longevity)
                         + software-defined vehicle (OTA updates, autopilot)"
      proof_point: "400+ mile range, Supercharger network, continuous improvement
                    via software, proven safety ratings, vertically integrated"

  positioning_rationale: |
    Tesla targets affluent early adopters rather than mass market because
    this segment values innovation and can afford premium pricing. They're
    opinion leaders who influence broader adoption.

    The benefit (performance + sustainability) directly addresses the historical
    compromise of EVs. Tesla proved EVs could be BETTER than gas cars, not just
    'good enough'. This reframed the entire category.

    Differentiation is defensible through years of battery R&D investment,
    manufacturing scale, charging infrastructure, and software expertise.
    Traditional automakers can't easily replicate this ecosystem.

  tagline_connection:
    tagline: "Accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy"
    relationship: "Mission-driven tagline reinforces positioning as not just car
                   company but sustainable energy company. Ambitious vision attracts
                   believers, not just buyers."
```

### 5.2 Value Architecture

**Format:** Three-layer value model

**Template:**
```yaml
value_architecture:

  functional_value:
    description: "[Tangible, practical benefits]"

    benefits: *
      - benefit_1:
          claim: "[Specific functional benefit]"
          metric: "[Measurable improvement if applicable]"
          proof: "[Evidence this is delivered]"

      - benefit_2:
          claim: "[Functional benefit]"
          metric: "[Quantification]"
          proof: "[Evidence]"

      - benefit_3: [...]

    performance_improvements:
      - "[Faster/Better/Cheaper dimension 1]"
      - "[Efficiency gain 2]"

  emotional_value:
    description: "[Feelings and psychological benefits]"

    feelings_delivered: *
      - feeling_1: "[e.g., 'Confidence']"
        trigger: "[When/how customer feels this]"

      - feeling_2: "[e.g., 'Delight']"
        trigger: "[When experienced]"

      - feeling_3: "[e.g., 'Peace of mind']"
        trigger: "[Context]"

    psychological_benefits: *
      - "[e.g., 'Reduced anxiety about X']"
      - "[e.g., 'Sense of control']"
      - "[e.g., 'Excitement and anticipation']"

    experience_quality:
      - "[e.g., 'Seamless and effortless']"
      - "[e.g., 'Delightful and surprising']"

  social_value:
    description: "[Identity, status, and belonging benefits]"

    identity_self_expression: *
      - "[What using this brand says about user]"
      - "[How it helps them express who they are]"

    community_belonging: *
      - "[What community they join]"
      - "[How brand creates connection]"

    status_recognition: *
      - "[How brand affects social standing]"
      - "[What it signals to others]"

  value_hierarchy:
    entry_value: "[Why people first consider brand - usually functional]"
    loyalty_value: "[Why people stay - often emotional + social]"
    advocacy_value: "[Why people recommend - deep value realization]"
```
