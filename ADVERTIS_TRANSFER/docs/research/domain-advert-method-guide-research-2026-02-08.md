### Tier 1: Identity & Positioning Frameworks (Partial — ADVERT Pillars A & D absorb)

**Kapferer's Brand Identity Prism (1986)**
Six facets: Physique, Personality, Culture, Relationship, Reflection, Self-Image. Organized on Sender/Receiver and Internal/External axes. Widely taught in business schools.
_What it covers:_ Brand identity structure and perception mapping.
_What it misses:_ No pricing, no products, no community mechanics, no risk assessment, no market validation. Purely identity-focused.
_Where ADVERT absorbs it:_ Pillar A (Authenticité) and Pillar D (Distinction) naturally cover all six facets while extending far beyond them.

Sources:
- [How Brands Are Built - Brand Identity Prism](https://howbrandsarebuilt.com/the-brand-identity-prism-and-how-it-works/)
- [Mindtools - Kapferer's Brand Identity Prism](https://www.mindtools.com/awqj2p3/kapferers-brand-identity-prism/)

**Aaker's Brand Equity Model (1991)**
Five dimensions: Brand Loyalty, Brand Awareness, Perceived Quality, Brand Associations, Proprietary Assets. Brand identity as combination of 8-12 elements across four perspectives (Product, Organization, Person, Symbol).
_What it covers:_ Measuring and building brand equity — the "value of a brand."
_What it misses:_ Descriptive, not prescriptive. Tells you WHAT to measure, not HOW to build. No operational guidance, no community, no ethics framework.
_Where ADVERT absorbs it:_ Pillar E (Engagement) via AARRR and KPIs, Pillar R (Risk) via SWOT on every variable, and Pillar T (Track) via market validation cover everything Aaker measures — plus actionable instructions.

Sources:
- [Indeed - Aaker Brand Equity Model](https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/career-development/brand-equity-aaker-model)
- [FourWeekMBA - Aaker Brand Equity Model](https://fourweekmba.com/aaker-brand-equity-model/)

**Keller's CBBE Pyramid (1993)**
Four levels: Identity (salience) → Meaning (performance + imagery) → Response (judgments + feelings) → Resonance (loyalty + community + engagement). Brand resonance = the ultimate level.
_What it covers:_ Building brand equity from customer's perspective, bottom-up. Resonance includes behavioral loyalty, attitudinal attachment, sense of community, active engagement.
_What it misses:_ Theoretical framework with no operational tools. How do you actually CREATE resonance? No process, no templates, no risk assessment.
_Where ADVERT absorbs it:_ The entire A→D→V→E cascade IS the operational path to Keller's resonance. ADVERT makes the pyramid actionable. [High Confidence]

_Where ADVERT absorbs it:_ Pillar A (Authenticité) includes vision/mission via Ikigai (which IS the "Why" but grounded in 4 dimensions, not 1), and Pillar D (Distinction) starts with personas — the "Who" Sinek ignores entirely.

### Ethics Framework — Persuasion vs. Manipulation

ADVERT explicitly integrates an ethics framework (the "Manipulation Matrix" from the brainstorm: Dealer vs. Facilitator). This is critically important because the methodology uses psychology-based techniques (Cialdini's influence principles, tribal mechanics, cult-building patterns) that sit at the boundary between persuasion and manipulation.

**The Ethical Line:**
- **Persuasion** = influencing through logical reasoning, emotional appeals, or storytelling with the consumer's awareness and consent
- **Manipulation** = covert or deceptive tactics to influence behavior without full awareness or consent

**ADVERT's Position:** The Manipulation Matrix (2x2: "Improves user's life?" x "Seller uses it?") explicitly forces consultants to classify their approach: True Facilitator, Cynical Facilitator, Delusional Dealer, or Pure Dealer. This self-assessment mechanism is a differentiator — most competing frameworks ignore ethics entirely.

**Vulnerable Populations:** Regulatory bodies worldwide flag targeting of vulnerable groups (children, elderly, financially distressed) as a primary ethical concern. ADVERT consultants working in African markets — where financial vulnerability may be higher — must be particularly vigilant. The guide should include explicit ethical guardrails.

**AI and Ethics:** As ADVERT evolves toward AI-powered SaaS, new ethical concerns emerge: "AI marketing together with personalization algorithms has deepened trust issues that now prompt new concerns about privacy violations, biased systems, and manipulation threats." The guide should address this for future-proofing.

### Advertising Standards Across African Markets

Consultants using ADVERT to develop brand strategies across Africa must navigate a fragmented regulatory landscape:

**South Africa (most mature):** The Advertising Regulatory Board (ARB) oversees advertising ethics, based on the International Code of Advertising Practice. Self-regulatory system with complaint mechanisms for misleading, offensive, or unethical advertising. Industry-specific regulations exist for pharmaceuticals, alcohol, tobacco, and financial services.

**Francophone Africa:** ARPP (Autorité de Régulation Professionnelle de la Publicité) provides the French self-regulatory framework, relevant to Francophone African markets. Founding member of EASA (European Advertising Standards Alliance).

**Pan-African Challenge:** No unified pan-African advertising standard exists. Each market has its own regulatory body (where one exists at all). Consultants must verify local requirements market-by-market.

**ADVERT Guide Implication:** The guide should include a "Regulatory Checklist" section per major African market in the field notebook appendix. The Risk pillar (R) should explicitly include advertising compliance as a standard SWOT variable.

**Notion/Spreadsheet Pain Points (The "Desired Pain" for SaaS Conversion):**

- **Scale collapse:** One brand = manageable. Five brands = painful. Daily operations = unsustainable

**Guide Design Implication:** The field notebook templates should be excellent enough to work well for 1-3 brands. The pain becomes evident at volume — exactly where the SaaS becomes desirable.

### Guide Structure Recommendations

Based on all research conducted, Mary recommends the following structure for the ADVERT Consultant Field Guide:

**Part I — The Foundation (Chapters 1-2)**
- Chapter 1: "Why Brands Fail" — The fragmentation problem, the superfan economy, the African opportunity. Hook the reader with the pain they already feel.
- Chapter 2: "The ADVERT Method" — Overview of the 7-pillar cascade (A→D→V→E→R→T→S). The sequential logic explained. Why this order matters. What makes it antifragile.

**Part II — The 6 Pillars (Chapters 3-8)**
Each pillar chapter follows a consistent structure:
1. **What this pillar does** — Simple, junior-friendly explanation
2. **The variables** — Each variable explained in ADVERT language (not academic jargon)
3. **How to fill it** — Questions to ask the client, data to collect, exercises to run
4. **Case study** — Real brand cult example illustrating the pillar (Apple for A, Nike for D, Patagonia for V, Harley-Davidson for E, etc.)
5. **Simple exercise** — Practical exercise the reader can do immediately
6. **"Pour aller plus loin"** — Expert sidebar referencing absorbed frameworks (e.g., "Experts will recognize Schwartz's Values Theory in how we approach brand values — ADVERT extends this by...")

- Chapter 3: **A — Authenticité** (Identity, Hero's Journey, Ikigai, Schwartz Values, Community Hierarchy, Timeline)
- Chapter 4: **D — Distinction** (Personas, Competitive Landscape, Brand Promises, Positioning, Tone of Voice, Visual Identity, Language Assets)
- Chapter 5: **V — Valeur** (Product Ladder, Value for Brand/Customer, Cost for Brand/Customer, Unit Economics)
- Chapter 6: **E — Engagement** (Touchpoints, Rituals, Community Principles, Gamification, AARRR, KPIs)
- Chapter 7: **R — Risk** (Individual SWOTs per variable, Global SWOT, Risk Score, Mitigation Priorities)
- Chapter 8: **T — Track** (3-Source Data Crossing, Validation, Market Reality Report, TAM/SAM/SOM)

**Part III — The Deliverable (Chapter 9)**
- Chapter 9: **S — Strategy** — How the 6 pillars converge into the 360° strategy document. How to assemble the deliverable. What the client receives. How it feeds production agencies and briefs. Short/medium/long term planning.

**Part IV — Ethics & Practice (Chapter 10)**
- Chapter 10: **The Manipulation Matrix** — Ethical framework. Persuasion vs. manipulation. Self-assessment tool. Regulatory awareness.

**Part V — The Last Page**
- One page: "You've mastered ADVERT. For those who do this daily, discover the tool that automates what you just learned to do by hand." — Link to SaaS. Nothing more.

**Appendices — The Field Notebook**
- Appendix A: Complete variable list with filling instructions
- Appendix B: Templates per pillar (worksheets, canvases)
- Appendix C: Interview question bank (what to ask the client at each pillar)
- Appendix D: SWOT generation template for Pillar R
- Appendix E: Strategy document template for Pillar S
- Appendix F: Regulatory checklists per major African market
- Appendix G: Data handling protocol for Pillar T

### Field Notebook Design Principles

Based on the EOS pattern research and Notion/Excel pain point analysis:

1. **Self-contained:** Each template works as a standalone worksheet — no dependencies on other tools
2. **Progressive:** Templates follow A→D→V→E→R→T→S order — each builds on the previous
3. **Clear instructions:** Every field has a "How to fill this" note — no ambiguity
4. **Client-facing language:** Templates use ADVERT terminology, not academic jargon — the client sees what the consultant sees
5. **Manually painful at scale:** Templates are excellent for 1 brand engagement. At 3+ brands running simultaneously, the consultant will FEEL the need for automation (cascade updates, SWOT generation, consistency checks)
6. **Print-friendly:** Designed to work when printed — consultants in Africa may not always have reliable internet
