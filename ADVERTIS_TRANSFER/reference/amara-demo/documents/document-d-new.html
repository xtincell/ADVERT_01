Our identity system isn't a costume‚Äîit's a translation. We take the warmth of African textiles, the geometry of traditional patterns, the earth tones of the continent's landscapes, and express them through modern design language.

Typography Guidelines

Hierarchy Best Practices

  • Limit hierarchy depth: Max 3-4 levels per page (H1, H2, H3, Body)
  • Use weight for emphasis: Bold for headings, Medium for emphasis, Regular for body
  • Maintain clear contrast: Size jumps of at least 1.25x between levels
  • Leading (line-height): Tighter for headings (1.1-1.3), looser for body (1.5-1.7)

Readability Requirements

  • Line length: 50-75 characters optimal (use max-width)
  • Paragraph spacing: 1.5em between paragraphs
  • Minimum body size: 16px (1rem)‚Äînever smaller
  • Color contrast: Text Primary on white = 10.5:1 (AAA)

Voice & Tone

AMARA's voice is the consistent personality behind all our words. Our tone adapts to context, but the voice remains: knowledgeable storyteller, warm guide, confident artisan advocate.

Voice Attributes

1. Storytelling-First

What it means: Every message has narrative depth. We don't just describe‚Äîwe explain the "why" and "who."

‚ùå Generic: "100% cotton blouse. Available in blue."
‚úÖ AMARA: "This indigo blouse carries the Yoruba Adire tradition‚Äîeach blue spiral hand-dyed by Folake in Abeokuta using cassava starch resist technique passed down from her grandmother."

What it means: We educate without being pedantic. Depth without jargon.

‚ùå Too academic: "The geometric motifs in this piece derive from pre-colonial Akan cosmology, specifically the Adinkra symbol 'Sankofa,' which epistemologically represents temporal recursion."
‚úÖ AMARA: "The bird symbol you see is 'Sankofa'‚Äîa Ghanaian Adinkra that means 'go back and fetch it.' It's a reminder that wisdom comes from learning from the past."

‚ùå Apologetic: "We know our prices are higher than fast fashion, but we think the quality is worth it."
