Risk S1: CAC Inflation Exceeds LTV Growth

Strategic High Priority
Risk Score
7.2/10

Description

Digital advertising costs (Facebook, Instagram, Google) are increasing 15-20% YoY. If our Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) grows faster than Customer Lifetime Value (LTV), unit economics become unsustainable.

Probability: 40%
Impact: High
Timeline: 12-24 months

Potential Impact

  • CAC exceeds $120 (vs. target $85), destroying unit economics
  • LTV:CAC ratio falls below 3:1 (vs. target 4:1+)
  • Forced to reduce marketing spend, slowing growth
  • Burn rate increases, runway shortens
  • Investor confidence erodes

Mitigation Strategies

1. Diversify Acquisition Channels (Primary)

Reduce dependence on paid ads. Target: 68% organic traffic by Year 3

  • ‚Ä¢ Build superfan referral program (each fan refers 3.2 customers)
  • ‚Ä¢ Invest in SEO/content (blog, cultural education, maker stories)
  • ‚Ä¢ Leverage PR and earned media (15+ placements Year 1)
  • ‚Ä¢ Community events and pop-ups (direct customer touchpoints)
2. Increase LTV Through Retention

Focus on repeat purchases and lifetime value growth

  • ‚Ä¢ Superfan program increases LTV from $340 to $890
  • ‚Ä¢ Subscription/membership model (exploring Year 2)
  • ‚Ä¢ Cross-sell and upsell strategies
  • ‚Ä¢ Exceptional customer service (NPS 78+ target)
3. Optimize Paid Performance

Maximize ROAS through better targeting and creative

  • ‚Ä¢ A/B test creative (storytelling vs. product-focused ads)
  • ‚Ä¢ Refine audience targeting (lookalike of superfans)
  • ‚Ä¢ Retargeting campaigns (lower CAC than cold traffic)
  • ‚Ä¢ Set hard CAC ceiling ($100) - pause if exceeded

Crisis Management

When a risk materializes into a crisis, rapid, coordinated response is critical. This section outlines our crisis management protocol.

Crisis Response Framework

Phase 1

Detection & Assessment (0-2 hours)

Actions:

  • Identify and confirm crisis (monitoring alerts, customer reports, team notification)
  • Assess severity (Low/Medium/High/Critical)
  • Activate crisis team (CEO, CMO, COO, Legal if needed)
  • Gather facts (what happened, impact scope, who's affected)
  • Document timeline

Deliverable: Crisis brief (1-page summary of situation, severity, initial facts)

Phase 2

Containment & Communication (2-8 hours)

Actions:

  • Stop the bleeding (halt operations if needed, pause marketing)
  • Internal communication (brief all team members)
  • Customer communication (email affected customers immediately)
  • Public statement (CEO statement within 4-6 hours if public crisis)
  • Monitor sentiment (social media, press, community reactions)

Communication Principles:

  • ‚Ä¢ Be fast (silence breeds speculation)
  • ‚Ä¢ Be honest (admit what we know and don't know)
  • ‚Ä¢ Be empathetic (acknowledge impact on people)
  • ‚Ä¢ Be accountable (own mistakes, no deflection)
Phase 3

Resolution & Recovery (1-7 days)

Actions:

  • Execute fix (repair, refund, replace‚Äîwhatever resolves the issue)
  • Customer remediation (make affected customers whole + extra)
  • Continuous updates (daily until resolved)
  • Monitor metrics (sentiment, NPS, churn, brand health)
  • Support team (crisis is stressful‚Äîcheck in on people)
Phase 4

Post-Mortem & Prevention (7-14 days)

Actions:

  • Conduct post-mortem (what happened, why, how we responded)
  • Identify root causes (not just symptoms)
  • Document lessons learned
  • Update processes to prevent recurrence
  • Share learnings (internal, potentially public if valuable)
  • Update risk register

Deliverable: Post-mortem report + updated processes
